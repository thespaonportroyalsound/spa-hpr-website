Rules & Regulations

Complete rules for residents, guests, and renters of The Spa on Port Royal Sound, HPR.

Critical Arrival Warnings & Banned Items

Important notice to all residents, guests, and renters: To preserve our community infrastructure and security, the following items and vehicles are strictly prohibited from entering or parking on the property. Violations will result in immediate towing or HOA fines. See the Fine Schedule (PDF) for details.

Strictly Prohibited Vehicles & Watercraft

  • Commercial Vehicles: No box trucks, wrapped commercial vans, or contractor vehicles parked overnight unless actively performing emergency work (water leaks, electrical issues, etc.).
  • Recreational Vehicles (RVs) & Campers: No RVs, motorhomes, travel trailers, or pop-up campers are permitted onto the property.
  • Watercraft & Trailers: Boats, jet skis, kayaks, canoes, and their associated trailers are prohibited from entering the property.
  • E-Bikes: Electric bicycles of any kind or classification are prohibited from entering the property.

Other Prohibited Items

  • Pets: The Spa, HPR does not allow pets onto the property. Service / Emotional Support Animals (ESAs) are allowed only when pre-registered with the HOA Office at least 10 days prior to the intended arrival date.

Parking & Gate Access

  • Two-Vehicle Limit: Only two vehicles are allowed per unit.
  • Parking Pass: Every vehicle must display either a valid parking pass on the dashboard, or a registered barcode sticker provided by the HOA office.
  • Parking Restrictions: There is no assigned parking on the property, but all residents and visitors are asked to park as straight as possible against the parking blocks and to respect any signage prohibiting parking (Fire Lanes, Trash Pickup Zones, etc.).

Documents Required for Resident and Contractor Decals

Owners or contractors registering for decals must provide the HOA Office with the following items:

  1. Valid Driver’s License
  2. Current Registration
  3. Insurance Information
  4. Vehicle’s year, make, model, color, and tag number (if not on registration)

Quiet Hours & Noise Compliance

Quiet Hours: 10:00 PM – 8:00 AM

All residents, guests, and visitors must exercise extreme care to avoid unnecessary noise, defined as noise that can be heard beyond the perimeter of the unit. Please be considerate of your neighbors, and do not play loud music, move furniture, or make any excessive noise during these hours.

Pools & Amenities

  • Indoor Pool / Fitness Center: Open 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM. These amenities require the blue-handled key to access the sides of the indoor pool, as well as an additional key for the fitness center door.
  • Outdoor Pools: The outdoor pools (“Family Pool” & “Beach Pool”) are open 8:00 AM – Sunset. These hours are regulated by the state of South Carolina.
  • Children: All children under 18 years old must be accompanied by their parent(s) or legal guardian(s). All residents are subject to the posted rules signage by each pool.

Trash & Waste Disposal

  • Household Trash: All household trash must be bagged, boxes broken down, and placed only inside of the designated community dumpsters. Trash may not be left on walkways or any other common areas on the property.
  • Bulk Items: Furniture, appliances, or construction debris may not be left in or around the dumpster areas. Residents are not permitted to use the “construction dumpster” for any purpose. That dumpster is for use by The Spa, HPR only.

Walkway & Railing Rules

  • No personal property may be placed in walkways at any time. Walkways are fire and safety egress routes, and items such as bags, carts, shoes, and doormats create a safety hazard.
  • Towels, bathing suits, and clothing may not be hung over railings. Hanging fabric over walkway or balcony railings traps moisture and damages the building’s structural materials.

Service / Emotional Support Animal Rules

  • The Spa, HPR does not allow pets on the property.
  • Service / Emotional Support Animals (ESAs) are allowed, but they must be pre-registered with the HOA Office at least 10 days prior to the intended arrival date.
  • Residents and their guests must fill out and submit the submittal form, as well as the required documents as requested on the form. (For example: Both service animals and ESAs must submit shot records, and those requesting an ESA must submit a letter from a physician recommending that their patient have an ESA.)
  • The Spa, HPR only recognizes and requests the documents listed on the application. We do not request or accept any certificates or similar identification in regard to the “service” status of any particular animal.
